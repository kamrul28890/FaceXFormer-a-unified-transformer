\begin{abstract}
FaceXFormer proposes a unified transformer for ten facial analysis tasks, but the public release omits the training code, dataset loaders, sampler, losses, and several implementation choices needed for full reproduction. This brief report summarizes an end-to-end reproduction effort: auditing the paper--code gap, reconstructing the architecture and training pipeline, implementing task-specific datasets and losses, and comparing released-checkpoint and trained-model behavior. The study finds that the released checkpoint supports partial verification, while faithful training reproduction remains limited by undocumented design choices, metric protocol ambiguities, and multi-task loss imbalance.
\end{abstract}
